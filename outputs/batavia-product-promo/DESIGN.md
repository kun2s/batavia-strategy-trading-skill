# Batavia Product Promo Design

## Style Prompt

A restrained finance-tech promo for an auditable crypto strategy control layer. The visual language is modernist, evidence-first, and slightly institutional: cream paper, deep ink, amber validation marks, and a red decision accent borrowed from the Batavia wordmark. Motion should feel like a receipt being compiled into a deterministic contract: clean pushes, block wipes, tracked data rows, and precise status stamps.

## Colors

- Background paper: `#F7F4ED`
- Deep ink: `#11263D`
- Near black: `#111111`
- Evidence amber: `#D99A2B`
- Decision red: `#D71920`
- Muted rule: `#D9D2C4`

## Typography

- Display and body: `IBM Plex Sans Condensed`
- Data and hashes: `IBM Plex Mono`

## Motion Rules

- Use medium-energy push transitions for related points.
- Use one staggered block wipe for the decision-status reveal.
- Entrances should be precise and staggered, with varied eases.
- Numbers and hashes use tabular numerals.

## What NOT To Do

- Do not use crypto neon, purple gradients, coin imagery, or trading-bot hype.
- Do not claim live execution, wallet access, or final historical performance.
- Do not show generic dashboards unrelated to the receipt contract.
- Do not replace the cream/deep-ink/amber/red palette with default blues.
